Gig Worker Social Security Fund Calculator

Platform-Specific Contribution Tracking

The tool is designed to calculate each platform's individual share of social security contributions for gig workers. This solves a major operational challenge:

The Multi-Platform Problem:

  • A delivery driver might work for Zomato, Swiggy, and Dunzo in the same month
  • A ride-hailing driver could be active on Uber, Ola, and local platforms
  • Each platform only sees their portion of the worker's activity

How this calculator helps:

Each platform can use this tool to calculate their proportional responsibility based on:

  • Number of workers engaged through their platform per month
  • The platform's share of social security contribution per worker
  • Their specific obligation regardless of what other platforms the worker uses

Clearing the confusion:

Instead of platforms arguing "this worker also works elsewhere, so it's not our responsibility," this system establishes that:

  • Each platform pays based on their usage of the worker
  • No double-counting or gaps - every platform pays their fair share
  • Transparent calculation - standardized method across all platforms
  • Scalable tracking - can handle workers across multiple platforms simultaneously

This is particularly crucial for implementing India's gig worker social security schemes where the law requires platforms to contribute, but doesn't clearly specify how to handle multi-platform workers. The calculator essentially creates a pro-rata system where each platform's contribution is proportional to their engagement with the worker.
